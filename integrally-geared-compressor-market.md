<p><p>Integrally geared compressors can be categorized into two primary types based on their operating principles: volumetric and velocity compressors. Volumetric compressors function by trapping a specific volume of gas and compressing it, making them ideal for applications requiring steady flow and high pressure. In contrast, velocity compressors rely on the kinetic energy of gas to achieve compression, offering higher flow rates and efficiency. Each type serves distinct industrial needs, influencing their adoption in various sectors like oil and gas, refrigeration, and chemical processing.</p></p>
