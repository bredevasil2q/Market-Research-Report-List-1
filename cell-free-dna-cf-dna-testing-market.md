<p><p>The Cell-Free DNA (cf-DNA) testing market is primarily categorized into three types: donor-derived cf-DNA, which is obtained from organ or tissue donors for transplant compatibility; circulating cf-DNA, released from healthy and tumor cells into the bloodstream, often used for cancer detection and monitoring; and cell-free fetal DNA, which is extracted from maternal blood to assess fetal health and genetic conditions. Each type serves distinct clinical purposes, enhancing patient care and diagnostic accuracy.</p></p>
