<p><p>The industrial films market encompasses various polymer types, each serving distinct purposes. LLDPE (Linear Low-Density Polyethylene) is flexible and tear-resistant, making it ideal for packaging. LDPE (Low-Density Polyethylene) is known for its clarity and durability, used in flexible packaging. HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene) is robust and moisture-resistant, suitable for containers. PET/BOPET (Polyethylene Terephthalate) offers high tensile strength and thermal stability, great for films and bottles. PP/BOPP (Polypropylene) is versatile for food packaging. CPP (Cast Polypropylene) is transparent and heat-sealable. PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride) is used for protective films, while Polyamide/BOPA (Biaxially Oriented Polyamide) provides high barrier properties for specialty applications.</p></p>
