<p><p>The glenoid cavity prostheses market is primarily divided into cemented and cementless types. Cemented prostheses rely on bone cement to secure the implant in place, offering immediate stability and is often favored for patients with good bone quality. In contrast, cementless prostheses use a porous surface that allows for biological fixation through bone ingrowth, promoting long-term stability and durability, making them suitable for younger, more active patients. Both types cater to specific clinical needs and patient profiles.</p></p>
