<p><p>The buckwheat market can be categorized into three main types: unhulled, raw, and roasted. Unhulled buckwheat includes the whole grains with their outer hulls intact, offering a higher nutritional value but requiring longer cooking times. Raw buckwheat refers to hulled kernels that are ready for consumption and cooking, providing a nutty flavor and nutritional benefits. Roasted buckwheat, often known as kasha, has been toasted to enhance its taste and aroma, making it a popular ingredient in various dishes and snacks.</p></p>
