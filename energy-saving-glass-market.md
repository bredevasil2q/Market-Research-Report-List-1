<p><p>The energy-saving glass market primarily includes hollow and vacuum types, designed to enhance energy efficiency in buildings. Hollow glass units consist of two or more glass panes separated by gas-filled spaces, reducing heat transfer and improving insulation. Vacuum glass takes this a step further by creating a vacuum between glass layers, significantly minimizing thermal conduction and convection, resulting in superior energy efficiency. Both types play a crucial role in sustainable architecture by reducing heating and cooling demands.</p></p>
