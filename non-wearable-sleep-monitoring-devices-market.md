<p><p>The non-wearable sleep monitoring devices market is primarily divided into two types: contact and contactless. Contact devices require physical interaction, such as mats or sensors placed under a mattress, directly measuring sleep patterns through movement and pressure. In contrast, contactless devices operate without physical touch, utilizing technologies like radar or infrared to monitor sleep by analyzing body movements and breathing rates from a distance. Both types aim to provide users with insights into sleep quality and duration, enhancing overall well-being.</p></p>
